Reflection — 2025-12-11

Lecture 17 — Independent Reading: Project Management as a Craft (Dr. Mike Clayton)

  • The reading described project management as a craft centered on people, expectations, and managing change.
  • I liked the Shu–Ha–Ri model because it makes mastery feel realistic: learn rules, adapt within them, then innovate.
  • I learned that professional PMs reduce failure by speaking clearly about risk and unrealistic constraints.
  • New idea: “speaking truth to power” is a skill—protecting the project sometimes means challenging the plan itself.
